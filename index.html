Importance and Virtue of Zakat

Zakat is a financial system in Islam that ensures the equitable distribution of wealth and social justice. It is mentioned 32 times in the Quran, highlighting its extraordinary importance. The purpose of Zakat is not only to help the poor but also to create balance in the economy and maintain the circulation of wealth. Today, poverty and economic inequality are increasing worldwide, but if every eligible Muslim pays Zakat honestly, no one will sleep hungry. Allah says: "Take from their wealth a charity to purify and sanctify them and pray for them; indeed, your prayer is a source of comfort for them." (At-Tawbah: 103) On the other hand, there is a severe warning for those who do not pay Zakat: "And those who hoard gold and silver and do not spend it in the way of Allah, give them tidings of a painful punishment." (At-Tawbah: 34) It is time that we consider Zakat not as a burden but as a social and spiritual investment, so that our society can become more just, stable, and prosperous.
